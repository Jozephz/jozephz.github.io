Black Hole Facts

  • You can’t directly see a black hole - Because a black hole is indeed “black” — no light can escape from it, it’s impossible for us to sense the hole directly
  • Black holes come in a range of sizes - There are at least three types of black holes, NASA says, ranging from relative squeakers to those that dominate a galaxy’s center.
  • Look out! Our Milky Way likely has a black hole - There is probably a huge supermassive black hole lurking in the middle of our galaxy. Luckily, we’re nowhere near this monster — we are about two-thirds of the way out from the center, relative to the rest of our galaxy.
  • Weird time stuff happens around black holes - When you’re at extreme speeds close to light. The black hole warps time and space so much that Unlucky’s time appears to be running slower.
  • Black holes are only dangerous if you get too close - Like creatures behind a cage, it’s okay to observe a black hole if you stay away from its event horizon, this zone is the point of no return, when you’re too close for any hope of rescue.
